A Beginner's Look at Japanese

The first step to learning the Japanese language is to learn the alphabet. Or, at least, to learn the sounds that exist in the language. There are absolutely no "tones" in Japanese like in many other Asian languages and there are only 2 exceptions within the alphabet which will be explained later. The Japanese alphabet does not contain letters but, instead, contains characters and, technically, they are not alphabets but character sets. There are three character sets: Hiragana, Katakana, and Kanji (Chinese characters). Hiragana is the main alphabet or character set for Japanese and Katakana is used for foreign words.

Vowels in Japanese

There are 5 vowels in Japanese. (a), pronounced "ahh", (i), pronounced like "e" in "eat", (u), pronounced like "oo" in "soon", (e), pronounced like "e" in "elk", and (o), pronounced "oh". All Hiragana characters end with one of these vowels, with the exception of (n). The only "consonant" that does not resemble that of English is the Japanese "r". It is slightly "rolled" as if it were a combination of a "d", "r", and "l".





ひらがな Hiragana

Hiragana developed from Chinese characters and were originally called 女で(onnade) or 'women's hand' as were used mainly by women - men wrote in kanji and katakana. By the 10th century, hiragana were used by everybody. The word hiragana means "oridinary syllabic script".

カタカナ Katakana

The katakana syllabary consists of 48 syllables and was originally considered "men's writing". Since the 20th century, katakana have been used mainly to write non-Chinese loan words, onomatopoeic words, foreign names, in telegrams and for emphasis (the equivalent of bold, italic or upper case text in English). Before the 20th century all foreign loanwords were written with kanji.

Japanese Phrases

English Romaji (Japanese with English characters) Japanese Audio
Thank you. Arigatoo gozaimasu. ありがとうございます。
Thank you very much. Doomo arigatoo gozaimasu. どうもありがとうございます。
You're welcome. Doo itashimashite. どういたしまして。
Please. Onegai shimasu. お願いします。
Yes. Hai. はい。
No. Iie. いいえ。
Excuse Me. Sumimasen. すみません。
I'm Sorry. Gomen’nasai. ごめんなさい。
I don't understand. Wakarimasen. わかりません。
I don't speak Japanese. Nihongo ga wakarimasen. 日本語がわかりません。
I don't speak Japanese very well. Nihongo wa amari joozu ja arimasen. 日本語はあまり上手じゃありません。
Do you speak English? Eigo o hanashimasu ka? 英語を話しますか？
Speak slowly, please. Yukkuri hanashite kudasai. ゆっくり話してください。
Repeat, please. Moo ichido onegai shimasu. もう一度お願いします。
